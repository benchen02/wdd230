Visual Hierarchy

Amazon

https://www.amazon.com

The reason why I chose Amazon.com for Visual Hierarchy is because they do really well in trying to catch your attention in what they want, specifically their service. The first thing you see when you open the page is a big advertisement about prime video then just above that you see where you can sign in if you have an account. Though when people go to Amazon.com, they already have an idea of what they want to buy, so a search button is placed at the very top followed by some categroies that may interest some people like deals or best sellers.

White Space and Clean Design

QuickBooks Online

https://quickbooks.intuit.com/oa/get-quickbooks

The reason why I chose QuickBooks Online is because just look at the amount of space they give everything. They use white space to make things look clean and clear. Now QuickBooks Online is an online accounting software so I do think that even though it may seem much is required so that when you're staring at the reports hours, it's easier on the eyes as well as making everything clear at a glance. Again, it may seem like it's alot of white space, but it's to make everything look clean and clear to the user.

PARC: Alignment

YouTube

https://www.youtube.com

I believe that youtube is a great example on Alignment in both the mobile web, app, and actual website. On the mobile and app, all the videos are centered and every page you may be looking for from the homepage to your subscription box is centered on the bottom. There's even a bit of friendly UI as it stays at the bottem of the screen instead of the bottem of the page. On the actual site, the pages you're looking for are from a collapsable menu on the side while the videos are in a grid to make it easier to see everything you need to know about the video.
